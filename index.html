About Renovion

A Lung Health Company Built Around the Patient

At Renovion, we treat people with diseases characterized by mucus, chronic inflammation, and an elevated risk of bacterial infection. Since our inception, we have been focused on clearing mucus and restoring the function of cilia — the microscopic structures that sweep pathogens and debris from the airway.

ARINA-1 has been shown to restore mucociliary clearance and ciliary function, downregulate inflammation, and provide a potent secondary antibacterial mechanism that protects the lungs from bacterial growth. Individuals who have used ARINA·1 report significantly improved quality of life and decreased mucus burden.

Headquartered in Chapel Hill, NC, Renovion is led by a multidisciplinary team with deep expertise across pulmonology, clinical development, chemistry, manufacturing, and operations.

Technology

ARINA·1 — Breaking the Vicious Cycle

A novel, at-home nebulized therapy that simultaneously addresses all three pillars of chronic airway disease: mucus clearance, pathological inflammation, and bacterial infection.

💧

Improves Mucociliary Clearance

Thins mucus viscosity and restores ciliary function and expression, re-enabling the airway's natural self-cleaning mechanism and preventing progressive mucus plugging.

8× more effective than hypertonic saline
🛡️

Decreases Pathological Inflammation

Decreases inflammation comparably to potent corticosteroids without harmful side effects — no evidence of neutropenia or increased infection risk, even with long-term use.

Steroid-equivalent efficacy, no steroid side effects
🦠

Inhibits Bacterial Growth

Broad-spectrum antibacterial activity covering Gram-positive (MRSA), Gram-negative (P. aeruginosa), and NTM bacteria (M. abscessus, MAC), without promoting resistance.

Broad-spectrum — no resistance promotion

Target Indications

Three Diseases, One Breakthrough Mechanism

In each of our target diseases, thick mucus accumulates in the airways, leading to mucus plugging, progressive lung damage, and difficulty breathing.

01

Lung Transplant

Post-transplant patients face chronic lung allograft dysfunction driven by mucus accumulation and dysregulated inflammation. ARINA·1 targets the underlying mucociliary pathophysiology to support graft longevity and quality of life.

Non-CF Bronchiectasis
